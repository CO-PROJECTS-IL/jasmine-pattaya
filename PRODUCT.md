# Jasmine (יסמין) — Israeli Restaurant in Pattaya

## Register

product

## Product Purpose

A PWA for Jasmine, an Israeli restaurant in Pattaya, Thailand. Three user types:
- **Customers**: scan a QR code at their table, browse the menu, and place orders
- **Kitchen staff**: see real-time order board to prepare food
- **Admin**: manage menu, employees, events, finances, and settings

## Users

- **Primary**: Israeli tourists and expats in Pattaya, ages 25-60. Dining out, often with family. Expect familiar Israeli restaurant experience with local Thai flair.
- **Secondary**: Kitchen staff — fast, glanceable order board on a mounted tablet.
- **Tertiary**: Restaurant owner/manager — full admin dashboard for business operations.

## Brand

- **Name**: יסמין / Jasmine
- **Personality**: Warm, inviting, premium but not pretentious. Israeli soul food meets tropical relaxation.
- **Colors**: Gold (#c9a84c) on dark (#080808). Evokes warmth, elegance, Middle Eastern hospitality.
- **Languages**: Hebrew (RTL, primary), English, Thai. Hebrew-first UI.
- **Vibe**: Upscale casual dining — not fast food, not fine dining. Think golden lanterns, dark wood, jasmine flowers.

## Anti-References

- Generic SaaS dashboards with blue/purple gradients
- Wolt/Uber Eats clone aesthetic (sterile delivery app feel)
- Over-designed restaurant sites with auto-playing videos
- Flat white backgrounds with thin gray text

## Strategic Principles

1. **Menu is king**: the customer menu must feel like browsing a beautiful physical menu, not scrolling a feed
2. **Speed for staff**: kitchen and admin screens prioritize function over form — fast, clear, zero confusion
3. **Cultural authenticity**: the design should feel Israeli and warm, not generic international
4. **Mobile-first**: 90% of customers will use phones. Desktop is secondary.
5. **Dark theme**: evening dining ambiance — the app should feel like being in the restaurant
